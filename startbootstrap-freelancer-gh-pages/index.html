About

I am a fifth year student studying Computer Science at the University of Cincinnati's College of Engineering and Applied Science. I have always had a deep penchant for intellectually challenging tasks, including mathematics and computer software development.

Co-op Experience

Over the Summer of 2019, I spent my time with Village Vancouver a non-profit in Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada. My chief task was to organize and pivot their technological situation in order to prepare for a new website that is still under current design. Not only that, but I was also tasked with managing all the Office 365 Users and doing a review+selection of a new mass-mailing list the organization could use to continue growing and servicing the people that it helps out weekly. I really appreciate the time I was able to have at work to independently research some very important tools/software such as Git, web browsers, and cloud computing. I am also really glad that I was able to fulfill a critical role for as long as I did. I learned a lot about non-profits, how they operate, and what it takes to coordinate enough people to run one. I hope that I will be able to assist other non-profits in the future, especially because of the unique goals they are able to complete.

Strengths

I am an Achiever, a person who stays very busy and endures long hauls of work. I have never compromised my productivity for my free time, and I am constantly trying to learn new things outside of work and school.

I am a Strategic person, I think on my feet and I am always keeping a watchful eye on the situation at hand. I believe that person resiliency is important for everyone and the need for it is inescapable. At my last job, there was a point in time when it appeared we didn't have enough office space to accomodate incoming hires, however within the afternoon I was able to re-arrange the space to make room as well as preserve ergonomics

I am an Individualization-based person, and I am very aware of the differences between the people around me. Depending on varying workflows and differing expectations of productivity, group work may proceed in a bad way if there isn't an effort being made by members to achieve higher consideration. By focusing on the nuances of the people around me, I can work more efficiently with them and also be a bridge between some of my peers. I use this skill everyday, especially when I am teaching chess to a classroom of elementary school kids.

I am an Input-based person, and I find that very often I am the locus of many different groups of people. I have received the most and the best help from people before so I strive to keep as many wonderful people around me as possible. Without my peers, I would not be in Computer Science right now, and I would not have as good of an idea about what I want from a future career.

I am a Learner, I never stop learning, and I am always trying to imporve the process by which I learn. The more efficiently I can learn things, the more time I get to practice those things, or to continue learning something new.
